## Notebooks

### 1. `Atom_in_a_thermal_cavity.ipynb`
**Solving the Lindblad equation for a two-level atom in a thermal cavity**

This notebook studies the dynamics of the density matrix $\rho$ of a two-level atom (ground state $|g\rangle$, excited state $|e\rangle$) coupled to a thermal cavity. The system is described by the Lindblad master equation, which accounts for both stimulated emission and photon absorption through jump operators $L_-$ and $L_+$.

Three numerical approaches are implemented and compared:

- **Runge-Kutta 4 (RK4)** — Direct numerical integration of the Lindblad equation. More accurate than a simple Euler scheme, which proves insufficient here.
- **Quantum Trajectory / Wave Function Monte Carlo** — Stochastic unravelling of the master equation. Individual quantum trajectories are simulated and averaged to recover the density matrix evolution. This approach scales more favourably than RK4 for large Hilbert spaces (cost $\sim N$ instead of $\sim N^2$).
- **Vectorization** *(in progress)* — Reformulation of the Lindblad equation as a linear system via vectorization of the density matrix.

Results from each method are cross-validated against one another, and against analytical solutions where available (e.g. spontaneous decay in vacuum).

### 2. `Chain_of_spins.ipynb`
**Entanglement entropy of a spin chain via Random Matrix Theory and Random Unitary Circuits**

This notebook computes the mean entanglement entropy of a chain of $N$ spin-1/2 particles (qubits) using two independent approaches, which are then compared.

- **Random Matrix Theory (RMT)** — Following Wigner and Dyson's framework, the Hamiltonian is replaced by a random matrix. Random quantum states are generated by drawing coefficients as i.i.d. random variables. The entanglement entropy is computed via Schmidt decomposition of the bipartite system (left $N/2$ vs right $N/2$ spins), and averaged over many samples. The theoretical maximum entropy $S = \frac{N}{2} \log 2$ serves as a reference.

- **Random Unitary Circuits (RUC)** — Starting from a product state, layers of random two-qubit unitary gates drawn from the Circular Unitary Ensemble (CUE) are applied alternately on odd and even bonds. The entropy $S_\infty$ (measured after a large number of time steps) is compared against the RMT prediction, demonstrating that both approaches converge to the same result.
